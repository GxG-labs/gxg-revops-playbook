# SEO/AEO/GEO Research Principles

## SEO

- Start with the reader problem, not keyword stuffing.
- Map one primary intent and several supporting intents.
- Prefer complete topical coverage over repetition of the same phrase.
- Include entities, synonyms, adjacent concepts, tools, metrics, and examples.
- Mark time-sensitive claims with a date or cautious wording.

## AEO

- Provide direct answers in the first useful paragraph of a section.
- Add definitions in plain language.
- Include FAQ questions that match real user phrasing.
- Use lists and tables where they help answer extraction.
- Avoid vague intros before answering the question.

## GEO

- Make facts easy to extract: definitions, comparisons, steps, checklists, examples.
- Add source-backed claims, numbers, named frameworks, named tools, and concrete caveats.
- Use consistent entity names throughout the article.
- Include "what it is not" blocks to reduce ambiguity.
- Build citation-worthy paragraphs: self-contained, specific, and not dependent on marketing context.

## Evidence Quality

Best sources:

- official documentation;
- academic papers;
- government or standards pages;
- product docs;
- reputable industry reports;
- primary interviews or first-party data.

Weak sources:

- unsourced listicles;
- vendor pages that make broad claims without data;
- outdated statistics;
- anonymous social posts.
