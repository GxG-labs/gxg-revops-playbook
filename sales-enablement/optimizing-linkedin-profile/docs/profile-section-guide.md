# LinkedIn Profile Section Guide

Use this for the section-by-section rewrite.

## Intro Card

### Name

Use the same name as resume, email signature, publications, and portfolio. Avoid decorative symbols.

### Photo

Use a recent, clear, face-forward photo. Avoid obvious AI portraits, old photos, group photos, sunglasses, heavy filters, or cropped event shots.

### Banner

Use the banner as positioning context. Good banners show:
- Domain: RevOps, data, engineering, GTM, sales, product
- Offer or audience
- Subtle proof: dashboard, product screenshot, speaking photo, city, industry

Avoid dense text because mobile crops aggressively.

### Headline

Two proven formulas — pick based on keyword density vs. readability preference:

**Formula A — Pipe-separated (higher keyword density):**
```text
{Target role/category} | {Specialty/domain} | {Core skills/tools} | {Proof/outcome}
```

**Formula B — Dot-separated (more readable, keyword-rich):**
```text
[Role] | [Keyword] · [Keyword] · [Keyword] | [Credibility anchor]
```

Career examples:

```text
RevOps Manager | B2B SaaS Pipeline & Forecasting | Salesforce, HubSpot, Attribution
```

```text
Senior Product Manager | B2B SaaS Growth | PLG · Experimentation · GTM | 3× pipeline growth at Series B
```

B2B examples:

```text
Fractional RevOps Lead | HubSpot + Salesforce GTM Systems | Pipeline Visibility for B2B SaaS
```

```text
AI GTM Consultant | Sales Research Automation & RevOps Workflows | Revenue Operations · CRM · Outbound Systems
```

For each headline option, include keyword reasoning:
- Why each keyword was chosen (recruiter search frequency, Sales Navigator filter alignment, or ICP language match)
- Which keywords are search-optimized vs. which are credibility signals

Avoid:
- Open to Work
- Founder
- Consultant
- Helping companies grow
- Ex-[company] only, with no role/category

## About

The first two lines must earn the click before "see more."

Structure:

1. Identity and audience
2. Problems solved
3. Proof bullets
4. Tools/domains
5. CTA or opportunity preference

Template:

```text
I help {audience} solve {problem} by {mechanism}.

My work spans {domains/tools}. I usually work where {context/trigger} creates {business pain}.

Selected proof:
- {Outcome/scope proof}
- {System/tool proof}
- {Leadership/client proof}

Open to {target roles/projects/intros}. Best way to reach me: {contact path}.
```

Rules:
- Write in first person if it sounds natural.
- Keep it plain and concrete.
- Mention target keywords naturally.
- Do not use corporate fog: passionate, dynamic, results-oriented, strategic leader, thought leader.

## Experience

Each relevant role should include:
- One context sentence
- 4-8 bullets for important roles
- Tools and methods
- Scope and outcomes
- **Exactly 10 attached skills per role** — use LinkedIn's exact skill nomenclature, not invented titles. Prioritize skills that align with target role/client keywords and are searchable in LinkedIn Recruiter and Sales Navigator filters.

Bullet formula:

```text
{Strong verb} {system/process/project} across {scope} using {tools/methods}, resulting in {outcome}.
```

If no metric exists, use one of:
- Named system
- Scope phrase
- Before/after change
- Complexity signal
- Stakeholder group

Examples:

```text
Redesigned HubSpot lifecycle stages and sales handoff rules across SDR, AE, and CS teams, giving leadership cleaner source-to-close visibility.
```

```text
Built an AI-assisted prospect research workflow that reduced rep prep time from 45 minutes to under 5 minutes per account.
```

## Featured

Use Featured for proof, not search.

Best items:
- Case study
- Portfolio
- GitHub or product demo
- Dashboard screenshot with anonymized data
- Sales/RevOps diagnostic
- Writing sample
- Talk, podcast, or webinar
- Certification or credential
- Offer page

Recommended order:
1. Best proof of outcome
2. Best proof of expertise
3. Best proof of trust
4. Best next step

## Recommendations

Ask for specific proof, not generic praise.

Request template:

```text
Could you write a short LinkedIn recommendation about our work on {project}? It would help if you mentioned the problem, what I did, the result, and what it was like working with me.
```

Build coverage:
- Former manager
- Peer/operator
- Client/customer
- Cross-functional stakeholder
- Direct report if leadership is relevant

## Licenses, Certifications, Projects, Publications

Add only if they support the target outcome.

For career:
- Certifications that appear in target job descriptions
- Projects showing target tools or domain
- Publications/talks that prove expertise

For B2B:
- Case studies
- Frameworks
- Public artifacts
- Talks/webinars
- Industry-specific credentials

## Services

Use for B2B/freelance/consulting mode when LinkedIn Services is available.

Make service names buyer-readable:
- RevOps Consulting
- HubSpot Implementation
- Salesforce Process Design
- Sales Enablement
- GTM Strategy
- AI Workflow Automation
